BITS connects Franklin University students with small-scale, real-world technology projects from nonprofits, small governments, and small businesses. Students gain applied experience while partners receive meaningful support.

Overview

Goal

Expand student career connectedness by delivering authentic, client-facing project experiences.

Who we serve

Nonprofits, small governments, and small businesses with practical technology needs.

How it works

Project intake → scope review → student onboarding → execution → deliverables → feedback.

Project Types

Web & Software

  • Website refresh, accessibility improvements
  • Simple web apps, forms, automation
  • Documentation and user training materials

Data, AI, Analytics

  • Dashboards and reporting
  • Data cleanup, pipeline basics
  • Marketing analytics insights

Cybersecurity

  • Security posture review (non-invasive)
  • Risk assessment and recommendations
  • Awareness materials and policy templates

Cloud & Infrastructure

  • Cloud migration planning (lightweight)
  • Configuration guidance and documentation
  • Cost and governance recommendations

For Partners

Partner responsibilities

  • Provide a clear project goal and context
  • Assign one primary point of contact
  • Participate in short check-ins
  • Provide feedback at the end

Recommended project scope

  • 2–4 weeks for small deliverables
  • 8–12 weeks for capstone-style work
  • Well-defined outcomes and constraints

We will help refine scope during intake.

For Students

What you gain

Portfolio artifacts, client communication practice, teamwork experience, and references.

Expectations

Professional conduct, timely communication, meeting milestones, and documentation.

Where it fits

Capstone courses, PF511, co-curricular projects, and special topic opportunities.
